# Sources of Help
Research notes for screen-sources-01 through screen-sources-03.
Must cover at least 4 distinct sources for full marks (8pts).

## 1. School Counselor
- Available during school hours, free, confidential
- Can help with stress, anxiety, depression, conflict, and more
- First stop for most students — easy to access
- How to access: ask a teacher or go to the guidance office

## 2. Trusted Adult (Parent, Guardian, Coach, Teacher)
- Someone who knows you and cares about your wellbeing
- Can provide support, perspective, and help connect you to resources
- Important: not all adults will react the same — pick someone safe
- How to access: reach out privately, start with "I need to talk"

## 3. Crisis Text Line
- Text HOME to 741741 (US) — free, confidential, 24/7
- Connects you with a trained crisis counselor via text
- No phone call required — good for those who struggle to talk
- How to access: text from any phone, any time

## 4. National Suicide Prevention Lifeline
- Call or text 988 (US) — free, confidential, 24/7
- For anyone in emotional distress or suicidal crisis
- Also available for people worried about someone else
- How to access: call or text 988 anytime

## 5. Therapist / Psychologist / Counselor
- Professional mental health support, ongoing relationship
- Can diagnose and treat anxiety, depression, and other disorders
- May require insurance or referral
- How to access: ask a parent/guardian or school counselor for a referral

## 6. Trusted Friend
- Peer support is powerful — someone your age who gets it
- Can reduce isolation and remind you you're not alone
- Not a replacement for professional help but a meaningful first step
- How to access: reach out — most people want to help if asked
