# Coping Strategies
Research notes for screen-coping-01 through screen-coping-03.
Must cover at least 5 distinct strategies for full marks (8pts).

## 1. Deep Breathing / Controlled Breathing
- Slow, intentional breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system
- Reduces heart rate and calms the body's stress response
- Technique: inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4 (box breathing)
- Use when: panic, anxiety, feeling overwhelmed

## 2. Physical Exercise
- Releases endorphins — the brain's natural mood boosters
- Reduces cortisol (stress hormone) levels
- Even a 20-minute walk can improve mood significantly
- Use when: feeling down, stressed, restless, or unable to sleep

## 3. Journaling / Writing It Out
- Puts thoughts into words, making them easier to process
- Helps identify patterns in mood and triggers
- Doesn't need to be formal — just getting it out helps
- Use when: overwhelmed, confused about feelings, anxious

## 4. Talking to Someone You Trust
- Sharing a burden reduces its weight
- Trusted friend, family member, or counselor
- You don't need advice — sometimes just being heard helps
- Use when: isolated, depressed, carrying something alone

## 5. Mindfulness / Being Present
- Focus on the current moment instead of past or future worries
- Can be as simple as noticing your surroundings for 2 minutes
- Reduces rumination (repetitive negative thinking)
- Use when: anxiety about the future, overthinking

## 6. Creative Outlets
- Art, music, writing, drawing — expressing emotion without words
- Provides a safe release for difficult feelings
- Builds a sense of accomplishment and identity
- Use when: frustrated, sad, need an emotional outlet

## 7. Setting a Routine
- Structure reduces uncertainty, which reduces anxiety
- Regular sleep, meals, and activity anchor the day
- Small wins (making your bed, eating breakfast) build momentum
- Use when: depression, low motivation, feeling out of control
